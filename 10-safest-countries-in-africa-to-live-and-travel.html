10 Safest Countries in Africa to Live and Travel. Africa is a continent of diverse cultures, landscapes, and wildlife.

It is also home to some of the most peaceful and stable countries in the world, according to the Global Peace Index (GPI).

The GPI is an annual report that measures and ranks the peacefulness of 163 countries based on three broad categories: ongoing conflict, societal safety and security, and militarization.

In this article, we will explore the 10 safest countries in Africa to live and travel, based on the latest GPI rankings for 2022.

If you are looking for a safe and comfortable place to live in Africa, you might want to consider these countries:

1. Mauritius:

Mauritius has a high level of human development, a stable democracy, a diverse and tolerant society, and a vibrant economy based on tourism, financial services, and agriculture.

Mauritius also boasts of beautiful beaches, coral reefs, mountains, and wildlife

2. Ghana:

Ghana has a long history of democracy, good governance, and respect for human rights.

It is also one of the fastest-growing economies in Africa, with abundant natural resources, a young population, and a dynamic cultural scene.

Ghana is known for its friendly people, rich traditions, diverse landscapes, and wildlife

3. Botswana:

Botswana is one of the most stable and prosperous countries in Africa, with a low level of corruption, a high level of human development, and a strong commitment to environmental conservation.

Botswana is famous for its stunning natural beauty, especially the Okavango Delta, the Kalahari Desert, and the Chobe National Park

To Travel

If you are looking for a safe and enjoyable place to travel in Africa, you might want to consider these countries:

4. Namibia:

It is a vast and sparsely populated country, with stunning landscapes, diverse cultures, and abundant wildlife.

Namibia offers many opportunities for adventure, such as sandboarding, skydiving, hiking, camping, and safari.

Namibia is also home to the oldest desert in the world, the Namib Desert, which features some of the highest sand dunes in the world

5. Senegal:

Senegal is a vibrant and colorful country, with a rich history, a lively music scene, and warm hospitality.

It has a variety of attractions, such as the historic island of Goree, the pink lake of Retba, the colonial town of Saint-Louis, and the coastal city of Dakar.

Senegal is also a gateway to explore other countries in the region, such as The Gambia, Mali, and Guinea-Bissau

6. Tanzania:

Tanzania is one of the most popular destinations in Africa for wildlife lovers, as it hosts some of the most spectacular natural wonders in the world, such as the Serengeti National Park, the Ngorongoro Crater, Mount Kilimanjaro, and Zanzibar Island.

Tanzania also has a diverse and friendly culture, with over 120 ethnic groups living harmoniously together

7. Zambia:

Zambia is a peaceful and friendly nation, with a stable democracy, a low level of corruption, and a high level of social cohesion.

Zambia is also blessed with natural beauty, such as Victoria Falls, the Zambezi River, the Luangwa Valley, and the Kafue National Park.

It is a great destination for wildlife enthusiasts, as it hosts a variety of animals, such as elephants, lions, leopards, giraffes, zebras, and hippos.

9. Equatorial Guinea

Equatorial Guinea is one of the richest countries in Africa, thanks to its oil and gas reserves.

Equatorial Guinea has a low level of violence, a high level of human development, and a moderate level of militarization.

It is also a culturally diverse country, with influences from Spain, France, Portugal, and various African ethnic groups.

Equatorial Guinea has some natural attractions, such as the Monte Alen National Park, the Bioko Island, and the Pico Basile volcano.

Sierra Leone is also rich in culture, history, and nature.

Conclusion

Africa is not a monolithic continent that can be easily stereotyped or generalized.

It is a continent of contrasts and complexities that offers many opportunities for living and traveling safely and enjoyably.
