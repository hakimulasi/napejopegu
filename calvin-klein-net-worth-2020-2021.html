Early Life

Calvin Richard Klein was born in New York on November 19, 1942. Calvin Klein was raised up in New York itself by Flo and Leo Stern. He was the second child out of three. He got the art of sewing from his grandmother, who was a seamstress. He used to watch her sewing very intently and eventually learned this art from her. Further, his mother and father encouraged him to continue his love and passion for this art of sewing.

While he was in high school, his friends were indulged in sports and physical activities, where he was interested in fashion designing and sewing. He got graduated from the Fashion Institute of Technology, New York.

Career

Calvin Klein started off his professional career in 1962 as an apprentice at a cloak and suite house where he used to work all day long and on weekends for more perfection in his work. He worked there for five long years. Later in 1968, Calvin Klein collaborated with his childhood friend Barry Schwartz to establish Calvin Klein Inc, which was just a coat shop at that time. A few years later, a huge order was given to him by Bonwit Teller. It was then when his company initially got a breakthrough.

Awards & Achievements

His work was highly demanded and later got popular throughout the world. Calvin Klein has been honored by various fashion awards and councils from all around the world. In 1973, He won his first prestigious “Coty Award” and won the title for three consecutive years from 1973 to 75 and became the youngest designer to achieve such a milestone.

Later the Councils of Fashion Designs of America awarded him three times in a row for his excellent work. In 1996, Times magazine listed him among 25 influential Americans. In 2002 Calvin Klein made a deal and sold his company to PVH that is Phillips Van Heusen Co.
