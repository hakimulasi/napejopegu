Mitch Benn Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Education

Mitch Benn, born in Liverpool, embarked on his educational journey at Dovedale Primary School, later moving to the Liverpool Blue Coat School, followed by the University of Edinburgh.

Here, his artistic talents blossomed, notably with the Edinburgh University Theatre Company at the Bedlam Theatre. He was also part of the Improverts, an improvised comedy troupe, which perhaps foreshadowed his future career in comedy.

Radio and Television Career

Benn’s radio and television career is nothing short of impressive. A regular on BBC Radio 4’s The Now Show from 1999 to 2016, and BBC Radio 2’s It’s Been a Bad Week between 2000 and 2006, he also contributed to Jammin‘ on BBC Radio 2.

Notably, he played a small role in Radio 4’s The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy in 2005 and regularly appeared on BBC Radio 2’s Steve Wright Show as the voice of Elvis in the Ask Elvis feature.

On television, Benn graced shows like Watchdog with a song about the PlayStation 3’s yellow light of death, and also made appearances on Live at Jongleurs and The Comedy Store.

Live Performances and Tours

He is no stranger to the live performance scene. He regularly plays at clubs and festivals across Britain and has toured globally, including in South Africa, Hong Kong, and Singapore.

His memorable run at the Edinburgh Fringe in August 2007, performing a two-hour show, The Mitch Benn Music Club, with his band the Distractions, was a highlight.

He also embarked on a UK national tour in December 2008, coinciding with the release of his sixth album, Sing Like an Angel.

In addition to his main career achievements, he has also been involved in other interesting projects. In 2010, he released I’m Proud of the BBC, a song that charted at 11th in the Independent Singles Chart.
