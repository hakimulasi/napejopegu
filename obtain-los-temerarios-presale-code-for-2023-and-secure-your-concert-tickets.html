About Los Temerarios

Originating from Fresnillo, Zacatecas, Los Temerarios are a well-known Mexican Grupera band that has its roots dating back to 1977 when brothers Adolfo Angel and Gustavo Angel, along with their cousin Fernando Angel, came together to form the musical group. Initially, they were identified as Conjunto La Brisa.

Throughout their successful career, Los Temerarios have released over 20 albums, earning significant recognition and accolades. Their achievements include two Grammy nominations, a Latin Grammy Award, an Excellence Award from Premio Lo Nuestro, and a Lifetime Achievement Award from the Latin Billboard Music Awards. In 2016, the band was honored with induction into the Latin Songwriters Hall of Fame.

They were also bestowed with the Best Mexican-American Album award at the inaugural Latin Grammy Awards in 2000 and received the Lifetime Achievement Award at the Premio Lo Nuestro 2005 Awards show. In 2012, their star was unveiled on the Las Vegas Walk of Fame, solidifying their position as enduring icons in Latin music.
