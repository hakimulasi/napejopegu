Frank Martin Early Life/Career

Early Life: - Martin, who grew up in Miami, is the son of Cuban political exiles and the family's first American-born member. In 1993, he earned a bachelor's degree in physical education from Florida International University. He worked as a bouncer at a local nightclub as one of his two jobs while attending FIU. He chose to become a full-time basketball coach in 1992 after being subjected to gunshots while on duty by a group of individuals he had expelled for fighting.

Professional Career: - From 2000 to 2004, Martin worked as an assistant coach/recruiting coordinator at Northeastern University. He transferred to the University of Cincinnati, where he played for Bob Huggins and Andy Kennedy for one season each. Martin joined Huggins' staff at Kansas State on April 5, 2006. In the aftermath of Huggins' departure, Martin was named head coach of the Wildcats on April 6, 2007, almost a year to the day after his arrival in Manhattan.
