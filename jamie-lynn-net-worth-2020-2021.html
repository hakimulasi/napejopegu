Early Life

Jamie Lynn Spears was born on April 4, 1991, in McComb, Mississippi, United States. Her father Jamie Spears, and her mother Lynne Spears got divorced in 2002. She is the sister of producer Bryan Spears, and the singer, dancer and actress Britney Spears.

Jamie Lynn Spears studied at the Kentwood High School, and the Parklane Academy in her hometown. She was a cheerleader for the basketball team of Parklane’s Academy.

Career

Jamie Lynn Spears debut movie was in 2002 alongside her sister Britney Spears in a comedy-drama titled “Crossroads”, the movie was commercially successful. In the same year, she began appearing in the sketch comedy show that aired on Nickelodeon TV “All That”, Jamie’s last appearance in the show was in 2004, and she received many great positive reviews.

After “All That”, Jamie Lynn Spears got into a deal with Nickelodeon for a comedy-drama series titled “Zoey 101”, she was the star of the series, and it contributed more to her successful career. Her sister, Britney Spears wrote “Follow Me” song and Jamie recorded it to be used as the series song. Jamie Lynn Spears had guest appearances in 2 series including “Just Jordan” and “Miss Guided”.

Jamie Lynn Spears released her first single song ever “How Could I Want More” in 2013, which later became a part of her first extended play album “The Journey”. The album was very successful as it peaked at five on the Top Heatseekers Albums chart in the United States, 24 on the Top Country Albums chart in the United States, and 25 on the Independent Albums chart in the United States. She also collaborated with her sister in a song titled “Chillin’ With You”, and with Tim Nichols and Connie Harrington on a song for Jana Kramer titled “I Got the Boy”.
