Career

In the year 2004, he started his career when he was chosen by producer Michael Picchiottino for the role of Adam Sheppard in the film ‘Clipping Adams.’ His debut was spectacular, winning him the ‘Best breakthrough performance’ award at Phoenix Film Festival.

He also started acting in TV commercials promoting brands like Progressive Insurance, Moviefone, and Kellogg. In the same year, he acted in the film ‘Sleepover’ and ABC series ‘The Days.’

In 2007 he did supporting roles in Films ‘American Crime’ and ‘Garden of the night.’ He also starred in many Theatre productions like Fagin in Oliver Twist. In the year 2010, he appeared as Todd Haynes, a supporting role in the film ‘Kick-Ass.’

His landmark achievement came with his role as a teenage murderer in the first season of the ‘American Horror Story’ of the FX anthology series. He starred as Kit Walker in the second season of the series titled ‘Asylum.’ This role earned him a nomination for Satellite Award for the best supporting actor. The third season of the same series saw him play the role of Kyle Spencer in ‘Coven,’ and in the fourth season titled ‘Freak Show,’ he played a circus performer with deformed hands.

In 2014 he starred in a comedy ‘Adult world,’ and in 2016, he played a role in the comedy ‘Elvis and Nixon.’
