Dogger Author Shirley Hughes Died Age 94, What You Need to Know About Her Family

July 2024 · 3 minute read

Shirley Hughes and her husband have three children together. She and her partner were married for over five decades. 

Hughes was an English author and illustrator who wrote picture books and children's literature. She had a seven-decade-long career, and she wrote more than sixty books, which have sold over 11.5 million copies.  

Shirley had illustrated over two hundred copies and was one of the most popular children's authors. She was awarded the Kate Greenaway Medal in 1977 and 2003. She wrote her first novel, Hero on a Bicycle, in 2015.

What Is Shirley Hughes Partner Or Husband Name?

Shirley Hughes' husband's name is John Sebastian Papendiek Vulliamy. He was an etcher and architect. She got married to her partner in 1952 and stayed married till 2007 till his death. 

When her husband passed away, she was doing okay during the weeks. However, the weekends felt like hell, which is why she began writing novels to make the days more bearable. She began enjoying writing and started doing more, reports Independent.

Hughes was a self-taught illustrator who began her career illustrating other authors' work. Her 1977 book Dogger was her breakthrough and won her a Kate Greenaway Medal. In 2007, it got voted the public's favorite Greenaway winner of all time.

She was born and raised in West Kirby, Wirral, England. She went to West Kirby Grammar School but left to join Liverpool School of Arts to study costume design and drawing. She also went to the Ruskin School of Drawing and Fine Art, Oxford.

Dogger Author Shirley Hughes Death At Age 94: How Did She Die?

Shirley Hughes died on February 25, 2022, at the age of 94 years. She passed away peacefully in her sleep at her home in London on Friday, reports Daily Mail.
